# AS-IS Process Analysis

## Module

Human Resource Management

---

# Business Objective

Manage employee information, attendance records, leave requests, and workforce reporting to support daily business operations.

---

# Current Process Flow

Employee Joins Company

↓

HR Creates Employee Record in Excel

↓

Employee Attendance Recorded

↓

Employee Requests Leave via Zalo

↓

Manager Reviews Request

↓

HR Updates Leave Balance in Excel

↓

Attendance Report Prepared

↓

Payroll Information Sent to Accounting

↓

Management Receives HR Report

---

# Process Analysis

| Step                        | User     | Current Tool |
| --------------------------- | -------- | ------------ |
| Employee Registration       | HR       | Excel        |
| Employee Information Update | HR       | Excel        |
| Leave Request               | Employee | Zalo         |
| Leave Approval              | Manager  | Zalo         |
| Attendance Tracking         | HR       | Excel        |
| Leave Balance Update        | HR       | Excel        |
| HR Reporting                | HR       | Excel        |

---

# Pain Point Analysis

## Pain Point 1

### Description

Employee information is maintained manually.

### Current Situation

Employee data is stored in multiple Excel files.

### Impact

* Data inconsistency
* Difficult information retrieval
* Increased administrative workload

---

## Pain Point 2

### Description

Leave requests are processed through Zalo.

### Current Situation

Employees send leave requests directly to managers.

### Impact

* No centralized tracking
* Approval history is difficult to audit
* Leave balances may be inaccurate

---

## Pain Point 3

### Description

Attendance information is maintained separately.

### Current Situation

Attendance records are collected and updated manually.

### Impact

* Time-consuming administration
* Increased risk of errors
* Delayed reporting

---

## Pain Point 4

### Description

HR reporting is manual.

### Current Situation

HR prepares reports using Excel.

### Impact

* Delayed management information
* Limited workforce visibility
* Additional administrative effort

# Key Findings

1. Employee information is decentralized.
2. Leave requests are processed manually.
3. Attendance information is not integrated.
4. HR reporting requires manual effort.
5. Management lacks real-time workforce visibility.

---

# AS-IS Summary

The current HR process relies heavily on Excel and Zalo communication.

The absence of a centralized HR system results in inefficient employee management, manual leave processing, delayed reporting, and limited workforce visibility.
