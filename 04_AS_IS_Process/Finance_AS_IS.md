# AS-IS Process Analysis

## Module

Finance Management

---

# Business Objective

Manage company revenues, expenses, receivables, payables, and financial reporting to support business operations and decision-making.

---

# Current Process Flow

Sales Completes Transaction

↓

Warehouse Delivers Product

↓

Accounting Receives Information

↓

Accounting Records Transaction in MISA

↓

Customer Payment Received

↓

Accounting Updates Receivable Information

↓

Expense Information Recorded

↓

Financial Reports Prepared

↓

Management Receives Financial Reports

---

# Process Analysis

| Step                 | User       | Current Tool |
| -------------------- | ---------- | ------------ |
| Revenue Recording    | Accounting | MISA         |
| Expense Recording    | Accounting | MISA         |
| Receivable Tracking  | Accounting | Excel + MISA |
| Payable Tracking     | Accounting | Excel + MISA |
| Cash Flow Monitoring | Accounting | Excel        |
| Financial Reporting  | Accounting | MISA + Excel |

---

# Pain Point Analysis

## Pain Point 1

### Description

Data must be entered multiple times.

### Current Situation

Sales, Warehouse, and Accounting maintain separate records.

### Impact

* Duplicate work
* Increased risk of errors
* Reduced productivity

---

## Pain Point 2

### Description

Receivable tracking is partially manual.

### Current Situation

Accounting monitors customer balances using Excel.

### Impact

* Delayed collection activities
* Limited visibility
* Increased credit risk

---

## Pain Point 3

### Description

Financial reporting is not real-time.

### Current Situation

Reports are generated after data consolidation.

### Impact

* Delayed management decisions
* Limited financial visibility

---

## Pain Point 4

### Description

Cash flow monitoring is manual.

### Current Situation

Cash flow information is consolidated in spreadsheets.

### Impact

* Delayed forecasting
* Limited planning capability

# Key Findings

1. Accounting uses MISA but remains disconnected from Sales and Warehouse.
2. Receivable and payable monitoring require manual effort.
3. Financial reports are not available in real time.
4. Cash flow visibility is limited.
5. Management reporting requires data consolidation.

---

# AS-IS Summary

The current finance process relies on MISA combined with manual Excel tracking.

The lack of integration between operational departments and finance creates duplicate data entry, delayed reporting, and limited financial visibility for management.
