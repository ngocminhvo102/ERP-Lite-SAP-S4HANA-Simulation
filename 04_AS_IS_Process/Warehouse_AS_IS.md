# AS-IS Process Analysis

## Module

Warehouse Management

---

# Business Objective

Manage inventory movements, warehouse operations, stock counting, and inventory visibility to ensure products are available for business operations while minimizing inventory discrepancies and losses.

---

# Current Process Flow

Sales Receives Customer Order

↓

Sales Contacts Warehouse

↓

Warehouse Checks Inventory in Excel

↓

Warehouse Confirms Stock Availability

↓

Warehouse Creates Goods Issue Record in Excel

↓

Warehouse Picks and Ships Products

↓

Warehouse Updates Inventory Spreadsheet

↓

Accounting Receives Inventory Information

↓

Management Receives Inventory Reports

# Pain Point Analysis

## Pain Point 1

### Description

Inventory information is not available in real time.

### Current Situation

Sales must contact Warehouse to verify stock availability before confirming customer orders.

### Current Process

Sales

↓

Call / Zalo Warehouse

↓

Warehouse Opens Excel

↓

Check Inventory

↓

Respond to Sales

### Time Required

5 - 15 minutes per request

### Impact

* Slow customer response
* Reduced productivity
* Risk of inaccurate inventory information

---

## Pain Point 2

### Description

Warehouse locations are not systematically managed.

### Current Situation

Products are stored based on employee experience and memory.

### Impact

* Longer picking time
* Difficulty locating products
* Increased operational inefficiency

---

## Pain Point 3

### Description

Inventory movements are manually recorded.

### Current Situation

Warehouse staff record inventory transactions in Excel after goods movement occurs.

### Impact

* Delayed inventory updates
* Increased risk of missing transactions
* Inventory discrepancies

---

## Pain Point 4

### Description

Physical inventory counting is difficult and time-consuming.

### Current Situation

Inventory counts are performed manually using printed inventory sheets.

### Impact

* Significant labor effort
* Inventory adjustment delays
* Reduced inventory accuracy

---

## Pain Point 5

### Description

Batch and expiry date management are not standardized.

### Current Situation

Batch information is maintained manually.

### Impact

* Difficulty tracing products
* Risk of expired products being shipped
* Regulatory compliance concerns

# Key Findings

1. Inventory data is maintained in Excel and updated manually.
2. Sales cannot view inventory information directly.
3. Inventory movements are not updated in real time.
4. Warehouse locations are not formally managed.
5. Inventory counting requires significant manual effort.
6. Product traceability is limited due to manual batch tracking.

---

# AS-IS Summary

The current warehouse operation relies heavily on Excel spreadsheets, manual communication, and manual inventory recording.

The lack of an integrated inventory management system results in limited visibility, inventory discrepancies, operational inefficiencies, and increased business risk.

An ERP solution is required to provide real-time inventory visibility, standardized warehouse processes, inventory traceability, and improved operational efficiency.
